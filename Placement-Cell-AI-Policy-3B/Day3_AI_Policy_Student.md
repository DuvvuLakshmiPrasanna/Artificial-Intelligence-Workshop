# 🎓 Student Academic Life — AI Use Policy

> **Version:** 1.0 · **Effective Date:** June 2026 · **Authored by:** Lab 3B Participant  
> **Scope:** All enrolled students using AI tools for academic assignments, exams, projects, extracurricular activities, and campus life.

---

## 📋 Scenario Classification

The following 8 scenarios represent common AI-assisted activities in a student's academic life, classified using EU AI Act–inspired risk tiers.

| Tier | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| 🟢 **Minimal** | Free use — no obligations or disclosures required |
| 🟡 **Limited** | Allowed with disclosure to teacher or mentor |
| 🔴 **High-risk** | Allowed only with mandatory safeguards and human review |
| ⛔ **Unacceptable** | Banned outright — violates academic integrity |

---

### Scenario Risk Classification Table

| # | Scenario | Tier | Reasoning |
|---|----------|------|-----------|
| 1 | AI used for doubt-clearing and concept explanation during self-study | 🟢 **Minimal** | Personal learning aid; no assessable output is produced; student drives understanding |
| 2 | AI-assisted grammar and clarity correction in student-written essays | 🟡 **Limited** | Allowed with disclosure; the ideas and arguments must originate with the student |
| 3 | AI-generated semester project report submitted as the student's own work | 🔴 **High-risk** | Report quality is the assessed skill; permitted only with full AI disclosure and faculty sign-off |
| 4 | AI-written code submitted for graded programming assignments | 🔴 **High-risk** | Coding logic is the assessed skill; permitted only with mandatory code walkthrough and oral defence |
| 5 | AI used to answer questions during an online proctored or take-home exam | ⛔ **Unacceptable** | Direct circumvention of assessment integrity; constitutes academic dishonesty |
| 6 | AI-generated fake internship certificate or experience letter uploaded to college portal | ⛔ **Unacceptable** | Fabrication of official credentials; a serious academic fraud offence |
| 7 | AI chatbot used to prepare for class presentations and group discussions | 🟢 **Minimal** | Preparation tool only; the student performs and speaks live with their own understanding |
| 8 | AI-generated scholarship application or personal statement submitted under student's name | 🔴 **High-risk** | Authentic student voice is the selection criterion; allowed only with mentor review and written declaration |

## ✅ Permitted Uses

The following uses of AI tools are explicitly allowed for students:

1. **AI may be used** to explain difficult concepts, theorems, and topics in simple language as a personal study companion, provided the student validates understanding through practice problems.

2. **AI may be used** to proofread and improve the grammar, flow, and structure of student-written drafts, provided all original ideas, arguments, and analysis belong to the student.

3. **AI may be used** to generate topic outlines, chapter summaries, and brainstorming mind-maps as planning aids before the student begins writing independently.

4. **AI may be used** to create personalised study timetables, revision schedules, and exam preparation plans based on the student's syllabus and deadlines.

5. **AI may be used** to review student-written code, suggest improvements, and explain errors — provided the student understands every change before submitting or deploying.

6. **AI may be used** to practise soft skills such as mock group discussions, debate preparation, and interview simulations for personal improvement.

7. **AI may be used** to summarise research papers and textbook chapters for personal comprehension, with the original source always read and cited in any submitted work.

---

## 🚫 Prohibited Uses

The following uses are explicitly prohibited, with a detection method for each rule.

---

**1. AI may NOT be used to answer questions, solve problems, or produce any response during a graded exam, test, or quiz — whether online or offline.**

> **Detection Method:** Proctoring software flags AI-tool tab-switching and copy-paste patterns during online exams. Faculty may also call for immediate handwritten re-tests if suspicious submission patterns are detected.

---

**2. AI may NOT be used to write, generate, or substantially produce a project report, assignment, or dissertation that is submitted for academic credit without prior written disclosure.**

> **Detection Method:** Faculty and department heads may require students to present an oral defence of submitted work, explaining methodology, data interpretation, and conclusions in their own words. AI-detection tools (Turnitin, GPTZero) are also applied to final submissions.

---

**3. AI may NOT be used to fabricate internship certificates, experience letters, skill badges, or any official document uploaded to the college portal or submitted to a recruiter.**

> **Detection Method:** All internship certificates and experience letters are verified directly with the issuing organisation by the academic office before being accepted into student records.

---

**4. AI may NOT be used to write scholarship application essays, statements of purpose, or motivation letters submitted as the student's authentic voice without disclosure.**

> **Detection Method:** Selection committees may conduct a short personal interview to verify alignment between the written application and the student's actual knowledge, experience, and communication style.

---

## ⚖️ Enforcement

The Academic Integrity Cell will conduct random oral assessments on project submissions — sampling 20% of students each semester — where students must independently explain and defend the work they have submitted. Violations will be handled in proportion to severity: a first offence results in a written warning and compulsory Academic Integrity Workshop; a second offence results in cancellation of the submission with a zero grade and a bar from co-curricular activities for one semester; a third offence is referred to the Disciplinary Committee for suspension or degree-level action. The college acknowledges that AI misuse in private study contexts cannot always be detected, and this policy therefore depends significantly on each student's personal honesty and the professional self-respect that comes from earning qualifications genuinely.
