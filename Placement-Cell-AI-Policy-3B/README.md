## 🎯 What This Policy Does

This document governs how students enrolled at the college may and may not use AI tools across their entire academic journey — from daily self-study to graded exams, project submissions, internship records, and scholarship applications.

It was designed using the **EU AI Act risk-tier framework**, applying structured reasoning to 8 real student scenarios rather than issuing a blanket ban or blanket permission.

## 🧠 Key Design Decisions

### Why Student Academic Life?
Students are the most direct and high-stakes users of AI in an educational institution. The scenarios span the full arc of a student's experience — from personal study habits to high-stakes assessment and career-defining documents. This makes the policy immediately applicable and personally relevant.

### Why not ban all AI use?
A blanket ban is unenforceable — students will use AI regardless. A well-structured policy channels AI use into transparent, verifiable behaviour, distinguishing between *learning aids* (low risk) and *assessment substitutes* (high risk). The goal is honest engagement, not surface-level compliance.

### Why does every Prohibited Use have a detection method?
A rule without a detection method is a wish, not a policy. Every prohibited use listed here has at least one practical, implementable way to detect or verify a violation. Rules that cannot be enforced were deliberately left out.

### Why a graduated 3-tier violation response?
A single punishment for all violations is unfair and produces worse outcomes. Warning → zero grade → disciplinary action makes the policy credible, proportionate, and legally defensible.

## 📖 EU AI Act Reference

This policy adapts the EU AI Act's four-tier risk model for an educational context:

| EU AI Act Tier | Policy Equivalent | Applied Meaning |
|----------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Unacceptable Risk | ⛔ Unacceptable | Banned outright — zero tolerance |
| High Risk | 🔴 High-risk | Allowed with safeguards + disclosure + human review |
| Limited Risk | 🟡 Limited | Allowed with transparency and disclosure |
| Minimal Risk | 🟢 Minimal | Free use — no obligations |
